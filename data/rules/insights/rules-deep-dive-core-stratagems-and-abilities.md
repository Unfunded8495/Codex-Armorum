This article is our deep dive on the core abilities and stratagems in 11th Edition, focusing on what’s changed in the new Edition.

This piece is aimed primarily at existing players, and we’re going to focus on what’s changed rather than digging into the full mechanics of the phase. If you’re a brand new player looking to dive into 11th Edition, we recommend you go and take a look at our main review, as that provides a full outline of how Warhammer 40,000 works on the tabletop!

As a note - for our review, we have been provided with access to the extended digital rules that will be made generally available in the Warhammer 40,000 app. This means that in a few places, we may talk about a rule or specific nuance that hasn’t been covered in previews or things you’ve seen elsewhere. We’ll try to flag the important ones!

## Stratagems

Stratagems as a concept haven’t changed that much. You still spend CP for effects and can’t use the same stratagem more than once per phase.

## One Stratagem Per Phase

A big change here is you cannot use multiple stratagems on the same units each phase, meaning you can’t do stuff like activate Heroic Intervention, then re-roll the Charge.

This one is going to trip people up a lot early on. It’s clearly here to stop some of the apocalyptic combos you could assemble with multiple buffs before, and it’s basically a good change, but until people assimilate it, they’re going to forget that Command re-roll is a Stratagem. This does also change the value of some Stratagems depending on timing - an Advance/Charge Stratagem you activate in the Movement Phase is now much better than one activated in the Charge Phase, because with the latter if you then flub the charge you’re invested in, you’re out of luck! It also raises the value of non-Stratagem abilities providing Charge re-rolls.

## Multiple Stratagem Costs

Some stratagems now have multiple modes, which let you spend more CP for better effects. Heroic Intervention is an example of this from the core stratagems, allowing you to countercharge into an aggressive foe for 1CP, or go on the offensive against someone cowering too close for 2CP.

## Unchanged Stratagems

The following Stratagems are unchanged:

- **Epic Challenge:** Allows one of your Characters to try and assassinate an enemy Character in melee via **Precision** (though how Precision works has changed, see below).
- **Insane Bravery:** Allows a unit to automatically pass morale, once per game.
- **Counter Offensive:** Allows a unit to interrupt the order of combat by temporarily gaining **Fights First**.
- **Grenades/Explosives:** Allows a unit to inflict Mortal Wounds by hurling explosives.
The rules for the last one are unchanged but Grenades is now called Explosives to be more inclusive of bundles of dynamite and the like (though is still compatible with the existing Keyword).

## Changed Stratagems

There are a few changes:

- **Command Re-Reroll:** Lets you re-roll a dice roll. You now re-roll just one die except when doing charges. This is a minor buff to all those 2d6 shot flamethrowers out there.
- **Crushing Impact:** Lets your Monsters/Vehicles crash into the foe for Mortal Wounds. This is the new Tank Shock. Monsters can do it now and on 1’s you do a mortal to yourself.
- **Rapid Ingress:** With the codification of Ingress Moves to the core rules, this now just lets you make one of those at the end of your opponent’s Movement Phase. It also can no longer target Aircraft and with the new rules for those, you probably didn’t wanna do that anyway.
- **Fire Overwatch:** Allows you to shoot at the end of your Opponent's Movement Phase. Similarly to Rapid Ingress, this gets an integration with a new core rule called Snap Shooting. When Snap Shooting, you resolve your shooting attacks as normal, except that you only hit on 6s, and cannot re-roll them. This makes non-Torrent options quite a bit weaker in Overwatch, as most strong Overwatch units relied on re-rolls to get a good number of hits. Weapons with **Sustained Hits** are your other good option to get a decent number of hits in. The timing of Overwatch has also been changed to the end of the Movement Phase, and there's no requirement to target an enemy that moved. This shakes up the dynamics a bit - you can no longer freeze all nearby enemies with a powerful Overwatch threat, as they can run and hide behind a wall, but it means that enemies that *do* stay still within sight (which can include enemy Monsters/Vehicles in combat) are potential targets. The unit firing Overwatch has to be unengaged, so your Monsters/Vehicles in combat still can't do it.
- **Smokescreen:** Lets a unit gain cover via triggering smoke launchers or grenades. With the new Cover rules, this now just gives you that. The thing is though, now the unit targeted by the Stratagem provides cover to the stuff behind the unit which is cool.
- **Heroic Intervention:** Allows you to perform a counter-charge at the end of the enemy's Charge Phase. This now triggers at the end of the Charge Phase, and improves in a number of ways. When you use it, you make a Charge Roll, and with the standard, 1CP version you can then select any number of enemy units that Charged within 12” as a Target. If you pay an extra CP, you instead cap your Charge roll at 6”, but can now charge *anything* within 6”. With Overwatch moving to movement phase only, this is your counterplay in the charge phase. It opens up a lot of options for armies that suffer from being move-blocked too, and makes it harder to steal objectives with pure movement, as opponents can use this to push back.
- **Go To Ground:** This one’s gone full stop. There is a rule of the same name now in the game but it’s part of the *Hidden* rule, giving you an extra -3” to detection range if every model is obscured by terrain

## Core Abilities

## What’s the Same

The following Core Abilities are unchanged from last edition:

- **Anti:** Weapons that are especially good against certain targets.
- **Assault:** Can be shot after advancing.
- **Close-quarters/pistol:** Can be shot in engagement (Close-quarters is a new identical ability that will gradually replace Pistol)**.**
- **Lance:** +1 to Wound when charging.
- **One-Shot:** Can be fired once per game.
- **Sustained:** Extra hits on a critical hit.
- **Twin-Linked**: Re-roll wounds.
- **Rapid Fire:** Extra shots within half range.
- **Torrent:** Hits automatically.
- **Feel No Pain:** A chance to ignore damage each time you suffer it.
- **Firing Deck:** An ability for transports allowing models inside to effectively shoot out of it.
- **Ignore Cover:** Does what it says on the tin.
There’s a pretty clear asterisk on that last one that now Ignores Cover affects your hit roll instead of your opponent’s save, but it still just ignores that mechanic.

## What’s Different

There are a few smaller changes that tighten things up or expand the scope of what things can affect.

- **Blast:** Extra shots targeting large units. Now has the X qualifier after, giving you that many shots per models in the unit.
- **Lethal Hits:** Automatic wounds on a critical hit. Is now optional, letting you save for a critical wound roll if you want to fish for Devastating wounds.
- **Devastating Wounds:** Deals Mortal Wounds instead of normal damage on a critical wound. Is mostly the same but has been rewritten to work like the rebalanced version of it at the end of 10th. They also all happen as a batch at the end of resolving your attacks which is a bit cleaner than before.
- **Infiltrators** (set up in no-man's land) and **Deep Strike** (Ingress anywhere on the Battlefield) have been rewritten to use 8” for their measurements, which puts them in line with the new charge rules.
- **Hazardous** simply makes you make a Hazard roll after using the weapon which makes sense.
- **Lone Operative:** Prevents you being targeted within a certain distance, 12" by default. Also gets the X qualifier, telling you how many inches away it has to be before it can be targeted.
- **Melta:** Extra damage in half range. Now changes the damage characteristic of the attack by the value, and also worth noting that the change in ordering to modifiers means that this no longer bypasses half damage or set-to-zero effects.
- **Indirect Fire** Allows a weapon to be used for Indirect Shooting.
- **Deadly Demise:** A chance for a unit to blow up when Destroyed. For Transports, now happens after any units Disembark and it was always weird that this wasn’t the case.
- **Extra Attacks:** Additional melee weapons used in addition to others. The Attacks value can now be modified! There are a small subset of units like Bloodcrushers with Skarbrand that get a decent bump from this. A few units like Kill Rigs have a *great* time in the Ork Waaagh as well.
- **Stealth:** Now provides you with Cover.
Now for the bigger changes:

- **Super-heavy Walker:** brings the Knight faction rule into the core rules. Letting you move these models through terrain more than 4” tall, risking Battle-shock on a 1.
- **Fight First:** Lets a unit Fight before units without this ability. Has no wording change, but plays very differently because of the updates to the Fight Phase. This means you no longer fight before your opponent, making this slightly less valuable.
- **Precision:** Attacks that can attempt to snipe or assassinate Characters in Attached Units. Now works by changing the allocation order at the start of the Allocate Attacks step, putting one Character group at the start of the order for the batch of attacks with Precision. This means that you’re committed to it for the whole batch of attacks, but it also means that if you’re using one of the abilities that hands it to a whole unit (e.g. the Stratagem in Lions of the Emperor) it suddenly becomes exceptionally deadly, because the Character you’re hitting is guaranteed to eat the worst saving throws. It does also mean that a given group of attacks can only ever kill one Character, as each Character is always in a separate allocation group.
- **Heavy:** Improved accuracy when stationary or moving slowly. This one’s a bit of a mixed bag. On the upside, it still applies to units where no model has moved >3”, so small repositioning to line up shots is possible. On the downside, if you are engaged, it does not apply, which means vehicles firing out of combat can no longer use it to mitigate the hit penalty for that.
- **Cleave X:** is new, and is melee Blast, only applies if you put all your attacks into a single target unit. This is a fantastic addition to the toolbox of abilities and something we’ve wanted to see for a while.
- **Hover:** is a new ability that lets you ignore the -2” payment to use FLY.
- **Psychic:** represents weapons charged with psychic energy. This is no longer just a downside for your opponent to get ways to mitigate your rules! You can ignore all WS/BS/hit roll modifiers, which is absolute fire with the new Cover rules.
- **Scouts:** Provides a pre-game move. There are two re-works here (and also now happens during resolve pre-battle abilities). The bad news for Space Marine fans - it no longer works if you are deployed outside of your deployment zone. However, it has a cool new option that if a unit with Scouts starts in Strategic Reserves, then at the start of the first Battle Round it can set up anywhere within your Deployment Zone. This can provide some powerful flexibility on scoring/utility units.
- **Surge Moves:** Allow a unit to move after being shot at, including into engagement. Now have their own little section and can no longer be done if you already moved this phase as well as forcing you to move as close to the target as possible.
- **Plunging Fire:** Improved accuracy from high ground. Now triggers at 3” and gives you +1 BS (countering cover, effectively). The move the 3” means it’ll now work with most of the terrain in the game and may actually come up during games.
- **Hunter X:** A totally new effect hidden deep in the digital rules. Hunter weapons specify a Keyword, and can *only* target units with that Keyword. We have zero examples of this in the wild as of yet, so we’ll have to wait and see what they do with this one.
- **Heal X:** Also covered in the digital rules, and seen on a previewed Stratagems for Chaos Daemons. Heal is a new rules effect that works like the current Necron Reanimation protocols. When you Heal a unit, you check if there are any models with missing Wounds, and recover one of those wounds if there are any. If there are not, you instead return one model to the unit with a single wound remaining. You then repeat this X times. If Heal specifies that it affects a single model, then only that model can recover wounds, and any excess are wasted.
