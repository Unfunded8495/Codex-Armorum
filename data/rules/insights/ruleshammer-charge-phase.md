## The Charge Phase \[11\]

Charging in 11th edition has some significant changes to be aware of, this guide covers the basics of the new charge phase rules but also aims to highlight areas that might cause confusion.The charge phase is broken up into 3 stages, though stage 11.02 represents the bulk of activity during the phase where units actually declare, attempt and make charges.

## Start of the Charge Phase \[11.01\]

This stage simply exists for “start of the charge phase rules” to be triggered. I’d expect to see more rules use this stage directly by name and number as the edition progresses but nothing much else has changed about it.

## Declaring Charges \[11.02\]

Here we start to see the big changes from previous editions. This step is split up into 3 distinct sub steps, but you go through each of them for every unit you’re charging with.

1. Declare Charge: Select one friendly unit that has not declared a charge this phase and is eligible to declare a charge (see below). That unit declares a charge.

A unit is eligible to declare a charge if it is on the battlefield, unless otherwise stated. Here are some rules that prevent a unit from being eligible to declare a charge

  - It is not within 12" of one or more enemy units.
  - It is engaged.
  - It made an advance or fall-back move this turn.
It’s important to note this is not an exhaustive list, there are abilities and rules that prevent charging spread through the faction rules but another prominent one is the very last effect of the Emergency Disembark 18.05 move

AFTER MOVING: Your unit is battle-shocked and, until the end of the turn, it is not eligible to declare a charge.

So you still can’t purposefully try to get your own transports destroyed, such as by taking damage from their own hazardous weapons or by collateral damage from the Crushing Impact stratagem, to get a unit closer to the enemy for a charge.

Another key point is that charges no longer declare a specific target at this step, you need eligible targets but you don’t need to choose a specific one. Also it’s possible for a target to be eligible but not actually “chargeable”, usually due to terrain.

![](ruleshammer-charge-phase-01.png)

Moving through terrain works the same in the charge phase as it does in general. I’d suggest a read of the Ruleshammer Terrain Guide if you’re unsure on what models can move through, over, or on top of terrain.

Once you have a unit you want to charge with, the next step is making a charge roll.

2. Make Charge Roll: Make a charge roll by rolling 2D6: the result is the maximum distance for the charge move.

There’s not much to say about this other than reminding players that this roll is now BEFORE you select charge targets.

## The Charge Move \[11.04\]

Yes the rule number is correct, it goes 01, 02, 04, then 03. The charge move is the bread and butter of the phase and it has some quirks to be aware of. The first two elements are fairly straightforward, Charge Moves are moves only units that have declared a charge can make, and they follow the same movement rules as described in section 03. It gets a bit more subtle at the Before Moving section though.

BEFORE MOVING: Select one or more enemy units that are within 12” of your unit and within the maximum distance of your unit, until the end of this move, each of those enemy units is a charge target.

The key thing to notice here is that the target has to be within the distance you rolled! So if a unit is 10” away and you roll a 9” charge then you can’t select that unit, even though 9” would be sufficient distance to reach the 2” engagement range.

This rule can significantly change the outcome of a charge, especially when terrain is involved. Here's an example of a monster/vehicle attempting two different charges into a unit behind a wall.

The next step is probably one of the most confusing ones. I’ve seen a lot of suggestions online that you are required to get to base to base contact when charging. An idea I think supported by the charge distance requirement of selecting a target, this is NOT the case. Whilst targets must be within that distance there is no requirement to end in base to base contact when making a charge move. There are other requirements though.

WHILE MOVING

  - Each model must end its move closer to one or more charge targets.
  - Each model that can end its move within 1” of one or more charge targets must do so.
  - Each mode that can end its move engaged with one or more charge targets must do so.

![](ruleshammer-charge-phase-04.png)

As you can see, no requirements to end as close as possible or in base to base contact, which is important as models in base to base contact with enemy models can’t make Pile In moves in the fight phase!

AFTER MOVING:

  - Your unit must be engaged with all of the charge targets.
  - Your unit cannot be engaged with one or more enemy units that are not charge targets.
  - Until the end of the turn, each model in your unit has the Fights First ability (24.13).

![](ruleshammer-charge-phase-05.png)

One last thing to note is that actually making a charge move is optional, you can decide not to move. Be aware though that declaring a charge is not sufficient to be chosen to fight in the next phase, units need to either be engaged with an enemy unit or have made a charge move to be selected to fight.

## End of the Charge Phase \[11.03\]

Once you’ve completed all your charge declarations and charge moves the phase comes to a close. Any rules that trigger “at the end of the charge phase” are resolved here.
