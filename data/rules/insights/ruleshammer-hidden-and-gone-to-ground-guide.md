*Welcome back to Ruleshammer! In this series we take a detailed look at the rules of key games, but Warhammer 40k in particular. In our second 11th edition article, we're looking at the new hidden and gone to ground rules from the new edition.*

Hidden is one of the new fundamental rules for Warhammer 40k 11th edition; this guide should bring you up to speed on how **Hidden** works as well as explaining the new **Gone To Ground** rule added in the 11th Edition Rules Commentary.

## How to Be Hidden 13.09

> A model is hidden while all of the following apply to it:

> - That model has the INFANTRY/BEASTS/SWARM keyword and is within a terrain area that contains one or more light/dense terrain features.
> - That model’s unit did not make one or more ranged attacks during this turn or during the previous turn.

This bit is fairly straight forward: Don’t shoot, be **INFANTRY/BEAST/SWARM**, and be within a **terrain area** that has at least one **light/dense terrain feature**, and the model gets to be **hidden**.

This version of the **hidden** rule is from the 11th edition rules commentary and is slightly different to the printed version in the Core Rules PDF. Models can now be **hidden** in **terrain areas** which only have **light terrain features**, compared to the original version in the core rules that only applied if the terrain area had any **dense terrain features**. If using the Games Workshop mission terrain layouts this change means that a model can be **hidden** in every **terrain area** on the board, none of them fail to meet this criteria. In fact **exposed terrain features** don’t appear to be used in the official layouts at all.

Note that you can be hidden during the first turn, there's a specific entry in the Rules Commentary that confirms that even though you haven't had a previous turn yet, rules that require "something to not have happened" still function. **Hidden** gets a specific mention.

## Hidden and the First Turn 13.09.01

> During the first turn, conditions that require something to not have happened during the previous turn are true. For the Hidden rule, this means that during the first turn a unit has not made ranged attacks during the previous turn.

## What Does Hidden Do?

While a model is hidden, it can only be visible to enemy models that are within its detection range. Unless otherwise stated, a model’s detection range is 15".

**Hidden** causes a model to count as **not visible** to **attacking models** outside of **detection range** regardless of if any **terrain features** or **obscuring terrain areas** area blocking the model from view. **Note that it's models not units**, it's possible for some models in a unit to not be hidden whilst others are. In scuh a case hidden will only be of benefit if the only models that would have been visible are hidden, if the unit can target any non-hidden models (i.e. they're visible and in range) then the rule offers no protection. **Detection range** is by default 15” but we’ve already seen a few rules that can increase this and decrease this in some of the Warhammer Community previews.

![](ruleshammer-hidden-and-gone-to-ground-guide-01.png)

Remember that this rule doesn’t change line of sight rules generally. If a **hidden** model is within **detection range** but **not visible** due to intervening **terrain features** or **obscuring terrain areas** then it remains **not visible** as normal.

![](ruleshammer-hidden-and-gone-to-ground-guide-02.png)

One thing to note here as well though is that detection range has limits in the Rules Commentary on how much it can be modified.

> Detection Range and Lone Operative can not be better than 9" or worse than 30".

## Gone to Ground 13.11.01

Gone To Ground adds additional interactions between Solid \[13.11\] terrain features and the Hidden rules. This rule can be found in the Rules Commentary in the 40k App, but here’s the text to aid explanation here.

> Hidden models that are within **Solid terrain features** gain an extra benefit while they have **gone to ground**.

This whole sentence is a bit odd already and probably needs a revision, models can't be "within" a **solid terrain feature**. They can be within a **terrain area** that has **solid terrain features** though, and I think it's pretty clear this is what's meant, but it's still odd to refer to them this way as Solid isn't a category of terrain feature. **Solid** is a rule ALL **dense terrain features** gain, you can read more about this in [the terrain guide.](#ruleshammer-terrain-guide)

> A model has **gone to ground** while all of the following apply

> - That model is **hidden**.
> - That model is **not fully visible** to the attacking model due to one or more intervening **solid terrain features**.
> - That model's unit did not make one or more ranged attacks during this turn or the previous turn.
> While a model has **gone to ground**, subtract 3" from that model's **detection range**. ***Designers Note:*** *Units that made ranged attacks in the current or previous turn cannot go to ground regardless of any abilities that allow them to shoot and remain hidden.*

Essentially this rule reduces **detection range** for models that are not fully visible to the attacking model. It applies to specific models against specific attacks. Note however that despite Hidden being extended to include light terrain features, this rule only applies "if a model is **not fully visible** due to one or more intervening **solid terrain features**". **Light terrain features** are not **solid** so they can't provide the **gone to ground** benefit.

![](ruleshammer-hidden-and-gone-to-ground-guide-03.png)

Because this rule relies on solid terrain features one of the key limitations you might encounter with it in your games is that quite often a unit that would have the **gone to ground** benefit, is **not visible** anyway because it’s entirely behind a **solid piece of terrain**.

![](ruleshammer-hidden-and-gone-to-ground-guide-04.png)

This does mean that unlike **hidden**, gone to ground is not a true or false state like being hidden is. It depends on the perspective of the attacking models and intervening solid terrain features.

![](ruleshammer-hidden-and-gone-to-ground-guide-05.png)
