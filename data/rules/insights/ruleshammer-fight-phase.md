Welcome back to Ruleshammer! In this series we take a detailed look at the rules of key games, but Warhammer 40k in particular. This week I’m looking at some tricky bits of the Fight Phase.

The Fight phase has had much of the same treatment as the other phases, it’s gained specific start and end steps with numbers of their own and it’s had significant elements codified into specific moves and types of fight. This article focuses on some of the more nuanced changes and questions you might find yourself asking as you work your way through the phase.

## PILE IN MOVES 12.02

In a significant change from previous editions, in 11th Pile In moves happen for all eligible units **BEFORE** any fights occur. Also activations for this step do NOT alternate between players, the active player makes all of their Pile In moves before their opponent makes theirs. Units can only make one Pile In move during this step.

## What units are eligible?

ELIGIBLE IF: It is the Fight phase and one or more of the following apply to your unit:

  - It is engaged.
  - It made a charge move this turn.
  - It was selected to make an overrun fight this phase (12.06).
This is pretty standard stuff, but the Overrun fight element of this rule does come out of the blue a bit, as Overrun fights aren't explained until later in the rules. At least in 11th we have handy rules numbers telling us where to go look for answers! I think the big question I've seen so far though is this.

## What if a Unit becomes eligible during the Pile In step after I’ve moved my other units?

This isn’t going to happen very often, and in the example below it’s something the Guard player almost definitely wants to avoid doing, but it can happen and I thought it worth exploring.

![](ruleshammer-fight-phase-01.png)

Whilst maybe a bit odd that the Immortals don't get to pile, the step is written without any swapping back to check for models that have become eligible during the step. They are eligible to fight but as only the Overrun fight type gets to make a Pile In Move they won't get to Pile In then either, they're not eligible to do an Overrun fight (read on for more detail on why that is).

## PILE IN MOVES TRIGGERING OVERRUN FIGHTS 12.06

Overrun fights are a whole new concept in 11th edition, with one of the most situational secondary triggers I think I've ever seen in the game. Just thinking up an example for when it might occur took some thinking!

ELIGIBLE IF: Your unit is unengaged, or was unengaged at the start of the Fight step but became engaged during the Fight phase.

Take specific note that the second criteria requires a unit that is unengaged at the start of the Fight **Step** not the phase. This is why the Immortals in the previous diagram would not be eligible to make an Overrun Fight, they would start the step engaged. If things had played out differently though, there’s a version of events where they would get to make an Overrun fight.

![](ruleshammer-fight-phase-02.png)

Remember that an Overrun fight is still a fight type meaning that the unit needs to be eligible to be selected to fight in the first place. To be eligible to fight a unit must meet one or more of the following conditions.

  - It is engaged, or it was engaged at the start of this step.
  - It made a charge move this turn
That this is checked at the start of the step is actually a significant change from 10th edition, as previously you could actually stop a unit from from getting to fight by removing all the models engaged with it. In the scenario in the diagram above, if this was 10th edition the Heavy Weapons Team wouldn't have gotten to fight at all, but in 11th they not only get to fight to get to make an Overrun fight!

## CONSOLIDATION 12.07

Consolidation like Pile In moves have been moved to a step of their own after all the Fights have been resolved, other than fights that consolidation can actually cause to happen that is. The consolidation move is pretty complex as it has several modes. The full move looks like this.

![](ruleshammer-fight-phase-03.jpg)

From Warhammer 40k 11th Edition - Pg42

Lets break down what these three modes really are fundamentally first.

**Ongoing Consolidations** - This mode is for units that are still engaged at the end of their fight, they represent models repositioning to continue the melee. If units are engaged at the start of this step they are required to choose this mode of consolidation.

**Engaging Consolidations** - This mode is fairly similar to consolidation moves of the past but slightly more restrictive still. Units that are unengaged but within 3” of any enemy units must select this mode and then end the move engaged with the units they selected when choosing it. If that unit or units hasn’t fought in the phase they get to do so now!

*There’s some speculation that the distance for this mode is meant to be 5” rather than 3”; this would prevent the issue I’m above to cover with the final consolidation type.*

**Objective Consolidation** - Selectable only if your unit doesn’t meet the criteria that require use of the first two modes, this mode allows a unit to consolidate towards and inside the area of an objective.

The issue though is that as written nothing prevents a unit from consolidating onto an objective that has enemy models outside of 3” on and ending that consolidation move within **engagement range** of that unit. We’re pretty sure here at TTB HQ that this isn’t intended to be allowed. Especially when **Engaging Consolidation** is the only mode that allows units to fight back when consolidated into.

![](ruleshammer-fight-phase-04.png)

The Tacoma FAQ did actually get released as this article was being written and whilst it didn't make any changes to the **Engaging Consolidation** distance it did rule that units that choose **Objective Consolidation** can not end that move engaged with enemy units. When this FAQ gets made part of the official rules we'll update this article to reflect that!
